1 · Employee & period

Each person logs c3 and c4 on two separate timesheets. One tool run = one reporting month.
Auto-set by name (Trooper 100%, Brian/Hiru 30%) — adjust if needed. Scales capacity and leave.
Public Holiday Personal Leave/Vacation Sick Marked days are auto-logged on the c3 timesheet's leave rows at standard hours × your time %, and excluded when monthly hours are spread across working days.

2 · Select activities by program

Two-step decision tree: pick the program group (mirrors Asana — Monthly Roundtable, Insurable AI, Liability, Gubernatorial Transition, Fundraising, Ops, plus New Area Exploration), then the kind of work. Each program has a General catch-all. Defaults follow the c3/c4 staff guidance; striped badges are gray areas and every entry's coding can be overridden in step 3.
Coding rules are built in — open for the quick reference (no doc lookup needed)

Code to c3 — educational, research-based, or non-legislative

  • Public thought leadership on whole-of-state AI governance — full and fair exposition, no call to action
  • Educational briefings & AI-fluency sessions (the general "here's the problem" version)
  • Supporting state agencies on non-legislative tools: oversight, procurement, licensing, insurance-commissioner guidance
  • Informing AGs about AI risks under existing law, without pushing a specific enforcement action
  • Helping officials implement existing laws (NY/CA transparency) via seminars and network-building
  • Non-partisan polling / message testing framed for public education, results broadly available
  • Model language only as technical assistance responding to a written request from a legislative body

Code to c4 — advocacy, lobbying, or aimed at specific legislation / political outcomes

  • Roundtable convenings and their prep — c4 by default (they touch specific legislation, policy models, enforcement)
  • Drafting or circulating model legislation / model language intended to influence a bill
  • Coordinating stakeholders to prepare specific legislation, or working with legislators to move it
  • Advocacy on specific liability or insurance frameworks for AI with lawmakers
  • Action-oriented convenings built around advancing a specific approach, not just explaining it
  • Bill-specific or non-public polling kept proprietary for friendly legislators
  • Capacity-building that advises on messaging or specific legislation rather than general risk education

Gray areas

Convenings, capacity building, and AG support flip on the same axis: educating / public research = c3; advocating a specific legislative or enforcement outcome = c4. Gubernatorial transitions: generic onboarding leans c3, pushing policy priorities leans c4. Genuinely mixed block → split it. Can't tell → code your best guess and flag it for review.

3 · Monthly hours, coding & flags

Enter time per activity either way — hours per working day (converted to a month total automatically) or the month total directly. Hours are spread evenly across the month's working days; any day is editable in step 4. Use Split for genuinely mixed work. Flag gray-area calls for review rather than leaving them uncoded.

4 · Review & direct edit

Full override: every cell, label and total below is directly editable before export. Daily values are an even allocation of your monthly actuals across working days — edit specific days where the real pattern materially differed. Rebuilding from monthly entries overwrites manual edits.

5 · Summary & generate timesheets

Check the month before exporting. Excel downloads reproduce the GDI template layout; "Save as PDF" prints both timesheets plus the flag summary in one landscape file. Flagged items and notes also land on a second sheet in each workbook.
Work saves automatically in this browser; use file save/load to move between computers or archive a month.
